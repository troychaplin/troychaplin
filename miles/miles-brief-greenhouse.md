## The concept

A WordPress block theme for a frontend developer's blog and portfolio, built around the metaphor of a greenhouse. Posts are cultivated specimens. The archive is a planting log. The whole site has a humid, patient, growing quality.

For eventual WordPress directory submission, so the metaphor should be strong enough to be memorable but loose enough that someone else's content could live in it.

## Where the weirdness lives

**Time and state language.** Drafts "germinating," posts "in bloom," archive as "harvested." That's the loud channel.

Color, type, and motion stay calm so the language carries the personality.

## Anchors, not a checklist

- Lifecycle-stage language for post states (germinating, growing, in bloom, harvested)
- A "currently in bloom" treatment somewhere
- Plant-illustration dividers used sparingly
- "Date planted" metadata
- An archive that reads like a planting log or seed catalog
- A palette of greens, frosted glass, terra cotta, and one flower color

## What I want from you first

Before any code:

1. **Pick a greenhouse.** Victorian conservatory with iron and glass? Modern botanical research facility? Backyard hobbyist? Rooftop urban farm? Each changes the mood.
2. **Typographic direction**, with a reason — botanical typography is its own subculture.
3. **A named palette** — colors named in the language of the garden.
4. **Three or four block patterns** that couldn't exist in any other theme.
5. **A first sketch of the homepage.**

Show me thinking before code. I want to react to direction before we commit.

## Constraints

- WordPress block theme, theme.json driven
- Directory-submission ready (GPL-compatible fonts, accessibility, no proprietary anything)
- Light and dark modes both — greenhouses know dawn, dusk, and grow-light hours
- The metaphor never breaks character, but it never winks at the camera either
