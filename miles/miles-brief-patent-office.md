## The concept

A WordPress block theme for a frontend developer's blog and portfolio, built around the metaphor of a patent office. Posts are filings. The archive is the patent register. Each piece feels like a documented invention with claims and figures.

For eventual WordPress directory submission, so the metaphor should be strong enough to be memorable but loose enough that someone else's content could live in it.

## Where the weirdness lives

**Document structure and figure conventions.** Patent numbers, FIG. callouts, claims lists, abstract treatments. That's the loud channel.

Color, motion, and texture stay restrained so the documents read as authoritative.

## Anchors, not a checklist

- "Filing" or "patent" as a name for posts (or simply the patent number)
- A figure-callout treatment for images (FIG. 1, FIG. 2)
- "Claims" as a stylized list-block pattern
- An abstract treatment derived from the post excerpt
- An archive that reads like a patent register
- A palette built from blueprint, vellum, and ink

## What I want from you first

Before any code:

1. **Pick a patent era.** 1880s mechanical illustrations? Mid-century industrial design? Contemporary tech filings? Each has a wildly different visual language.
2. **Typographic direction**, with a reason — patent documents have specific typographic conventions worth knowing.
3. **A named palette** — colors named in the language of the office.
4. **Three or four block patterns** that couldn't exist in any other theme.
5. **A first sketch of the homepage.**

Show me thinking before code. I want to react to direction before we commit.

## Constraints

- WordPress block theme, theme.json driven
- Directory-submission ready (GPL-compatible fonts, accessibility, no proprietary anything)
- Light and dark modes both — clerks work all hours
- The metaphor never breaks character, but it never winks at the camera either
