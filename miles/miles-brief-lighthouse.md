## The concept

A WordPress block theme for a frontend developer's blog and portfolio, built around the metaphor of a lighthouse keeper's log. Posts are watches. The archive is the logbook. Solitary, rhythmic, observational — the keeper's relationship to time and weather mirrors a writer's relationship to practice.

For eventual WordPress directory submission, so the metaphor should be strong enough to be memorable but loose enough that someone else's content could live in it.

## Where the weirdness lives

**Voice and rhythmic metadata.** Numbered watches, weather conditions, vessels-passed counts, watch-handover language. That's the loud channel.

Type, color, and motion stay disciplined so the log feels like a real working document.

## Anchors, not a checklist

- "Watch" as a name for posts (numbered)
- Per-post metadata that reads like a log entry — weather, time, conditions
- A "currently on watch" treatment somewhere
- An archive that reads like a logbook
- A palette of gray sea, brass, weathered paper, and one signal color — but make a real choice

## What I want from you first

Before any code:

1. **Pick a station.** Maine 1880s? Scottish North Sea? Pacific Northwest mid-century? Automated modern relic still being kept by hobbyists? Each changes the mood.
2. **Typographic direction**, with a reason — keeper's logs have specific handwriting and stamp conventions.
3. **A named palette** — colors named in the language of the coast.
4. **Three or four block patterns** that couldn't exist in any other theme.
5. **A first sketch of the homepage.**

Show me thinking before code. I want to react to direction before we commit.

## Constraints

- WordPress block theme, theme.json driven
- Directory-submission ready (GPL-compatible fonts, accessibility, no proprietary anything)
- Light and dark modes both — keepers stand night watches
- The metaphor never breaks character, but it never winks at the camera either
