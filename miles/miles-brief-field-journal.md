## The concept

A WordPress block theme for a frontend developer's blog and portfolio, built around the metaphor of a working naturalist's field journal. Posts are observations. The archive is the journal's index. Experiments are sketches in progress.

For eventual WordPress directory submission, so the metaphor should be strong enough to be memorable but loose enough that someone else's content could live in it.

## Where the weirdness lives

**Hand-drawn warmth and observational metadata.** Hand-ruled margins, pencil lines, sketch dividers, dated and located entries. That's the loud channel.

Color, type, and motion stay measured so the journal feels like a real working document, not a costume.

## Anchors, not a checklist

- "Observation" or "entry" as a name for posts
- Date + location metadata that feels journaled, not bloggy
- Sketched dividers and margin-note treatments
- Specimen-style figure callouts for images
- An archive that reads like a journal's table of contents
- A palette of paper, pencil, ink wash, and one specimen color — but make a real choice

## What I want from you first

Before any code:

1. **Pick a naturalist.** Botanist? Ornithologist? Entomologist? Marine biologist? Each has its own visual language — pressed flowers vs feather studies vs pinned insects vs tide-pool sketches.
2. **Typographic direction**, with a reason — there's a hand-lettered impulse to either resist or embrace, but pick one.
3. **A named palette** — colors named in the language of the discipline.
4. **Three or four block patterns** that couldn't exist in any other theme.
5. **A first sketch of the homepage.**

Show me thinking before code. I want to react to direction before we commit.

## Constraints

- WordPress block theme, theme.json driven
- Directory-submission ready (GPL-compatible fonts, accessibility, no proprietary anything)
- Light and dark modes both — fieldwork happens dawn to dusk
- The metaphor never breaks character, but it never winks at the camera either
