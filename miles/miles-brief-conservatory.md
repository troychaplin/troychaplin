## The concept

A WordPress block theme for a frontend developer's blog and portfolio, built around the metaphor of a music conservatory. Posts are compositions. The archive is a recital program. Movement markings, key signatures, and performance metadata give the chrome its voice.

For eventual WordPress directory submission, so the metaphor should be strong enough to be memorable but loose enough that someone else's content could live in it.

## Where the weirdness lives

**Musical metadata and classical typography.** Movement markings, key signatures, duration-as-reading-time, opus numbers. That's the loud channel.

Color, motion, and texture stay restrained so the typography and metadata can carry the personality.

## Anchors, not a checklist

- "Composition" or "piece" as a name for posts
- Movement markings (Allegro, Adagio, Andante) as tone tags
- Reading time framed as duration
- Opus-number-style post IDs
- Sheet-music-inspired dividers used sparingly
- A palette of ivory, ink, deep red velvet, and brass — but make a real choice

## What I want from you first

Before any code:

1. **Pick a conservatory.** 19th-century European with marble busts? Mid-century American jazz school? Contemporary chamber music society? Modern Berklee-style? Each changes the formality and palette.
2. **Typographic direction**, with a reason — classical music has specific typographic traditions worth knowing (think G. Henle Verlag, Bärenreiter).
3. **A named palette** — colors named in the language of performance.
4. **Three or four block patterns** that couldn't exist in any other theme.
5. **A first sketch of the homepage.**

Show me thinking before code. I want to react to direction before we commit.

## Constraints

- WordPress block theme, theme.json driven
- Directory-submission ready (GPL-compatible fonts, accessibility, no proprietary anything)
- Light and dark modes both — concert halls dim for performance
- The metaphor never breaks character, but it never winks at the camera either
