## The concept

A WordPress block theme for a frontend developer's blog and portfolio, built around the metaphor of a meteorological observation station. Posts are observations. The archive is a log. The site's personality borrows the *vocabulary* of weather instruments — gauges, barometers, sparklines, isobars, station tags — and uses them as decorative and structural language rather than literal data.

For eventual WordPress directory submission, so the metaphor should be strong enough to be memorable but loose enough that someone else's content could live inside it without feeling like a costume.

## Where the weirdness lives

**Data visualization as decoration.** Hairline instruments, sparklines, contour lines, station metadata. That's the loud channel.

Typography, color, and motion stay quieter so the instruments can sing. One loud channel, three quiet ones.

## Anchors, not a checklist

- "Observation" as a name for posts — or something better you find
- Timestamped + located metadata that feels instrumental rather than bloggy
- A "current conditions" treatment somewhere on the site
- Sparklines or gauges as visual rhythm — decorative or content-driven, your call
- Archive views that read like a weather log
- A palette with sky, paper, and ink in it — but make a real choice, don't default to safe neutrals

## What I want from you first

Before any code:

1. **Pick a kind of station.** Coastal lighthouse? Alpine observatory? 1940s met office? Modern airport tower? Antarctic research base? It changes everything — voice, palette, density, mood.
2. **Typographic direction**, with a reason behind the pairing.
3. **A named palette** — colors named in the language of the metaphor.
4. **Three or four block patterns** that couldn't exist in any other theme.
5. **A first sketch of the homepage.**

Show me thinking before code. I want to react to direction before we commit.

## Constraints

- WordPress block theme, theme.json driven
- Directory-submission ready (GPL-compatible fonts, accessibility, no proprietary anything)
- Light and dark modes both — weather has hours
- The metaphor never breaks character, but it never winks at the camera either
