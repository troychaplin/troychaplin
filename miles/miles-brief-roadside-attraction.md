## The concept

A WordPress block theme for a frontend developer's blog and portfolio, built around the metaphor of a mid-century roadside attraction. Posts are stops. The archive is a road map. The site greets you like a neon sign greets a tired driver.

For eventual WordPress directory submission, so the metaphor should be strong enough to be memorable but loose enough that someone else's content could live in it.

## Where the weirdness lives

**Sign-painter typography and warm voice.** Big confident display faces, hand-painted feel, status toggles styled as neon. That's the loud channel.

Color and motion stay disciplined so the type carries the personality.

## Anchors, not a checklist

- A status toggle somewhere (VACANCY / NO VACANCY style) for "now writing" or availability
- Big sign-painter display headers
- Section-as-stop language ("the diner," "the lookout," "the gift shop")
- Hand-painted divider energy without becoming literal
- An archive that reads like a road map or attraction directory
- A palette of neon, dust, and motel cream — but make a real choice

## What I want from you first

Before any code:

1. **Pick a region and decade.** 1950s Route 66 motel? 1960s Catskills resort? 1970s desert truck stop? Tiki bar? Each is a different mood.
2. **Typographic direction**, with a reason — display type *is* the personality here.
3. **A named palette** — colors named in the language of the road.
4. **Three or four block patterns** that couldn't exist in any other theme.
5. **A first sketch of the homepage.**

Show me thinking before code. I want to react to direction before we commit.

## Constraints

- WordPress block theme, theme.json driven
- Directory-submission ready (GPL-compatible fonts, accessibility, no proprietary anything)
- Light and dark modes both — neon needs night
- The metaphor never breaks character, but it never winks at the camera either — kitsch should be earnest, not ironic
