## The concept

A WordPress block theme for a frontend developer's blog and portfolio, built around the metaphor of a 1970s hotel lobby. The site *is* the lobby; you've checked in; the chrome plays the role of staff. Warm, generous, slightly ceremonial.

For eventual WordPress directory submission, so the metaphor should be strong enough to be memorable but loose enough that someone else's content could live in it.

## Where the weirdness lives

**Spatial role-play and warm color.** Lobby-as-homepage, concierge nav, guest-book comments, key-tag post IDs. That's the loud channel.

Typography and motion stay measured so the warmth feels generous, not loud.

## Anchors, not a checklist

- "Room" or "key" as post identifiers
- Concierge-style navigation
- A check-in or arrival treatment on the homepage
- A guest-book treatment for comments or testimonials
- An archive that reads like a registry or directory
- A palette of warm browns, mustard, brass, and conversation-pit cream

## What I want from you first

Before any code:

1. **Pick a hotel.** Luxury chain? Beach resort? Mountain lodge? Urban boutique? Roadside motor inn? Each changes everything.
2. **Typographic direction**, with a reason — 1970s hospitality typography is specific and worth getting right.
3. **A named palette** — colors named in the language of the lobby.
4. **Three or four block patterns** that couldn't exist in any other theme.
5. **A first sketch of the homepage.**

Show me thinking before code. I want to react to direction before we commit.

## Constraints

- WordPress block theme, theme.json driven
- Directory-submission ready (GPL-compatible fonts, accessibility, no proprietary anything)
- Light and dark modes both — lobbies are open 24 hours
- The metaphor never breaks character, but it never winks at the camera either — period warmth, not period parody
