## The concept

A WordPress block theme for a frontend developer's blog and portfolio, built around the metaphor of a museum specimen cabinet. Posts are specimens. The archive is a wall of drawers. The site feels like quietly inspecting curated objects under glass.

For eventual WordPress directory submission, so the metaphor should be strong enough to be memorable but loose enough that someone else's content could live in it.

## Where the weirdness lives

**Curatorial structure and labeling.** Latin-ish binomials, specimen tags, collection metadata, drawer-like archive views. That's the loud channel.

Color, motion, and texture stay restrained so each specimen reads cleanly.

## Anchors, not a checklist

- "Specimen" or similar as a name for posts
- A binomial-style subtitle convention (e.g., *Tutorialis reactica*)
- "Collected on" and "habitat" as metadata fields
- Hairline rules and ample paper-white space
- An archive that pulls open like cabinet drawers
- A palette of paper, ink, and a single museum-display accent

## What I want from you first

Before any code:

1. **Pick a museum.** Victorian natural history with brass fittings? Mid-century anthropology with concrete walls? Contemporary minimalist with white plinths? It changes everything.
2. **Typographic direction**, with a reason — labels and binomials want a specific kind of voice.
3. **A named palette** — colors named in the language of curation.
4. **Three or four block patterns** that couldn't exist in any other theme.
5. **A first sketch of the homepage.**

Show me thinking before code. I want to react to direction before we commit.

## Constraints

- WordPress block theme, theme.json driven
- Directory-submission ready (GPL-compatible fonts, accessibility, no proprietary anything)
- Light and dark modes both — galleries have evening hours
- The metaphor never breaks character, but it never winks at the camera either
