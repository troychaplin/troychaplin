## The concept

A WordPress block theme for a frontend developer's blog and portfolio, built around the metaphor of an observatory. Posts are observations. Archives are observing sessions. The whole site speaks of patience, dark skies, and the slow work of looking carefully.

For eventual WordPress directory submission, so the metaphor should be strong enough to be memorable but loose enough that someone else's content could live in it.

## Where the weirdness lives

**Celestial metadata and dark-as-default.** Coordinates, observing-session timestamps, star-chart elements, lunar-phase indicators. That's the loud channel.

Typography, color, and motion stay disciplined so the universe doesn't feel decorative.

## Anchors, not a checklist

- "Observation" or "session" as a name for posts
- RA/Dec-style coordinate metadata
- Star-chart or constellation dividers used sparingly
- A "currently observing" or "tonight's session" treatment
- An archive that reads like an observing log
- A palette of deep night, brass, and one star-color — but make a real choice

## What I want from you first

Before any code:

1. **Pick an observatory.** 1900s Mount Wilson with brass and wood? Contemporary remote rig? Amateur backyard setup? Radio telescope array? Each changes the mood.
2. **Typographic direction**, with a reason — astronomy has its own typographic traditions worth knowing.
3. **A named palette** — colors named in the language of the night sky.
4. **Three or four block patterns** that couldn't exist in any other theme.
5. **A first sketch of the homepage.**

Show me thinking before code. I want to react to direction before we commit.

## Constraints

- WordPress block theme, theme.json driven
- Directory-submission ready (GPL-compatible fonts, accessibility, no proprietary anything)
- Light and dark modes both — but dark is the true state; light is the daytime view of the dome
- The metaphor never breaks character, but it never winks at the camera either
